  text=Emphasis and Strong Emphasis
  text=The two basic inline decorations are italic and bold. You can also write them with underscores: italic and bold. Both forms are equivalent in CommonMark.
  text=Nesting is where things get interesting. You can have bold containing italic text or italic containing bold text. Parsers must handle the open/close delimiters correctly even when they interleave.
  text=Triple delimiters give you bold italic all at once — equivalent to ***text***. That's the same as **_text_** or *__text__*.
  text=Edge Cases
  text=An asterisk at the start of a word: emphasis right next to a comma, like this, should close cleanly.
  text=Emphasis that spans a long phrase with multiple words should still apply across all of them without breaking at spaces.
  text=You can have nested strong inside em or nested em inside strong. The renderer must keep both decorations active simultaneously.
  text=Strikethrough is a GFM extension covered in a later sample, but it is worth noting that it does not nest naturally with emphasis in some parsers.
  text=An underscore in the middle\_of\_a\_word requires escaping if you don't want emphasis. Without escaping, middle_of_a_word is treated as plain text because intraword underscores are not emphasis delimiters in CommonMark.
  text=Bold text followed immediately by italic with no space — the renderer must not merge these into one run.
  text=A single asterisk at end of sentence like this* is not emphasis because the left-flanking delimiter rule is not satisfied.
