  text=Links with Titles
  text=Inline links can include an optional title in quotes after the URL. The title typically appears as a tooltip on hover in HTML renderers:
  text=Kotlin language site
  text=Android Studio download
  text=Titles can also use single quotes:
  text=Jetpack Compose
  text=Or parentheses:
  text=Material Design 3
  text=Reference Links
  text=Reference-style links separate the link destination from the usage. This keeps long URLs out of the prose and makes text more readable:
  text=I recommend reading [the Kotlin docs][kotlin] and checking out [the Compose samples][compose-samples].
  text=Reference IDs are case-insensitive and can be reused:
  text=Visit [Kotlin][kotlin] multiple times without repeating the URL.
  text=Collapsed and Shortcut Reference Links
  text=When the link text matches the reference ID you can use a collapsed reference: [kotlin][] — just brackets with no ID.
  text=Or a shortcut reference: [kotlin] — just the text in a single pair of brackets.
  text=Empty Titles
  text=The title attribute may be an empty string: example. This is technically valid though uncommon.
  text=Title Escaping
  text=Titles may contain escaped quotes: tricky. The parser must handle the backslash-escape within the title string.
